Light and Sound in the Environment (Science, Social Studies)

  Big Ideas:

  • Understanding Natural Phenomena as light and sounds being fundamental aspect of the natural world
  • Light is required to see
  • Technological innovations involving light and sound have an impact on the environment
 Learning Objectives:
The students will be able to:
  • Describe properties of light
  • Explain how light moves and how it changes
  • Explain the terms reflection, refraction and absorption
Specific Expectations:
  • Describe properties of light, including that light travels in a straight path and light can be absorbed, reflected and refracted
  • Assess the impacts on society of devices that uses properties of light or sound or both
Cross Curricular Connections:
  • Social studies
  • STEM: The STEM infusion will be engineering as students will be designing and building a mirror maze to get light travel to an object
  Instructional Strategies:
Observation, Recording data, Hands-on activities such as conducting experiments and creating model, Think-Pair-Share

Learning Activities

Engagement:

Light Show- The light will be turned off and use a light projector onto the ceiling. After they have spent a little time observing, ask a question, “How does the light get from this machine to the ceiling? What do you think is happening?

Define the terms using a flashlight and book, water, glass etc.

  • Reflect- When light bounces back of the object
  • Refract- the bending of light when it passes from one transparent material to another
  • Opaque- not able to see through ,the light cannot pass through
  • Translucent-able to see through an item, the light can partially pass through.

Activity- Building light maze

(https://www.beano.com/games/lazer-maze) A virtual version of light maze will be projected on the board. Each group will create one mirror maze and they are asked to share their creation with whole class.

Exploration:

Take a nature walk around the school grounds. Encourage students to observe and listen for different sources of light and sound.

Explanation:

Facilitate a class discussion about how light interact with objects in the environment. Discuss concepts such as absorption, reflection (Click to watch video)

Extension:

Have students create a collage or presentation showcasing the sources of light they observed during the nature walk.

Stop Motion Animation Project - Usage of Digital Tool

Light: Divide students into small groups and assign each group a specific aspect of light to focus on (reflection, refraction, absorption, etc.). Have them create stop motion animations that demonstrate the behavior of light in different scenarios. They can use materials like clay, paper cut-outs, or digital drawing tools to create their animations.

Differentiation

Universal Design for Learning (UDL): Provide audio descriptions or captions for visual materials. Offer choice in how students present their observations (e.g., written report, oral presentation, multimedia project).

English Language Learners (ELL): Provide picture dictionaries or real-life objects to help students identify and label sources of light -natural or artificial. Pair ELL students with fluent English speakers during the nature walk for support.

Light sources-natural/artifical- Worksheet

Special Education: Offer additional sensory experiences during the nature walk (e.g., feeling different textures, listening to recordings of nature sounds). Provide graphic organizers or sentence starters for students to organize their thoughts.

Special Education: Offer additional scaffolding or one-on-one support during hands-on activities. Provide alternative assessment methods such as visual posters or oral presentations.


Assessments
Formative:

Observe student engagement and participation during the nature walk. Use a checklist to assess understanding of light interactions in the environment.

Summative:

Evaluate collages or presentations for accuracy and completeness. Use a rubric to assess students' ability to identify and describe sources of light as well as their understanding of how they interact with the environment.
