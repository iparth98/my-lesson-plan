The Impact of Light and Sound on our Environment

  Big Ideas

  • Light and sound affects the habitats and life of animals
  • The engineering process can help us find solutions to problems.
 Learning Objectives:
The students will be able to:
  • Research the problems related to sound and light in our environment
  • Define Light Pollution and Sound Pollution
  • Describe problems with light pollution and sound pollution
Specific Expectations:
  • Assess the impacts on society of devices that use the properties of light or sound, or both
  • Assess the impacts on the environment of light energy and sound energy
  • Describe how different objects and materials interact with light and sound energy
Cross Curricular Connections:
  • Language: Oral & written communication
  • Math: Measurements while doing designs, builds and experiments.
  • Social Studies: Use the social Studies inquiry
  Instructional Strategies:
Classroom discussions, Small group discussions, giving each other peer feedback, Student self-assessment using the criteria for success

Learning Activities

Guiding Questions:

  • What is sound and light pollution and how does it affect living things?
  • How do machines that make sounds and lights affect living things?

Prior knowledge:

  • Light travels in a straight line
  • Light is reflected and absorbed differently by various surfaces
  • Sound travels in a straight line
  • Sound is reflected and absorbed differently by various surfaces
Engagement:

Start with a discussion on everyday experiences with light and sound. Encourage students to share examples from their surroundings.

  • Discussion as a class: Can you think of times when light/sound bothered you? (bright light or loud music)
  • What did you do? (Responses could be -put on sunglasses, close the blinds to sleep in the dark, turn down the music, and close a window)

Show images or videos depicting instances of light and sound pollution in urban and natural environments. LP-5 Images to prompt discussion

Building vocabulary with discussion such as pollution, pollute, litter, conservation, wavelength, light, sound, noise, brightness, loudness dampening, headset, sunglasses, and blinds/curtains can be written on the chart or on virtual word wall. Pollutionword sort- Worksheet

Exploration:

Divide students into small groups and provide them with case studies or scenarios related to light and sound pollution. Have each group research and analyze the causes, effects, and potential solutions for their assigned scenario.

5_Light sound pollution- Worksheet

Encourage hands-on exploration by conducting simple experiments to demonstrate the effects of light and sound pollution (e.g., measuring light intensity or sound levels in different settings).

Explanation:

Facilitate a class discussion where each group presents their findings from the exploration activity. Summarize the common causes and effects of light and sound pollution identified by the groups. Provide additional information on the scientific principles behind light and sound pollution, including how they propagate and interact with the environment.

Extension:

Engage students in a creative project where they design posters or presentations to raise awareness about light and sound pollution in their community.

Organize a field trip or virtual tour to observe light and sound pollution in different settings, such as urban areas, parks, or industrial zones.

Invite guest speakers, such as environmentalists or city planners, to share their experiences and insights on mitigating light and sound pollution.

Differentiation

Universal Design for Learning (UDL): Offer content in multiple formats (text, audio, visual), Provide options for expression and engagement, Foster a collaborative learning environment.

English Language Learners (ELL): Use visual aids and real-life examples, Provide language support tools (bilingual dictionaries, glossaries), Offer scaffolded instruction and peer support.

Special Education: Individualize accommodations based on IEPs or 504 plans, Use multi-sensory instruction with tactile materials, Provide alternative assessment options and collaborate with support staff.

These strategies aim to ensure that all students, regardless of their diverse learning needs, can access and engage with the lesson content effectively.


Assessments
Formative:

Observe and assess students' participation and engagement during class discussions and activities. Review students' responses to reflection questions or exit tickets to gauge their understanding of key concepts. Provide constructive feedback during group work sessions to guide students in their exploration and analysis.

Summative:

Assign a project where students develop a comprehensive plan to address light and sound pollution in a specific environment (e.g., a city, a national park, a residential neighborhood).

Evaluate students' projects based on their understanding of the causes, effects, and solutions related to light and sound pollution, as well as the clarity and effectiveness of their proposals.

Administer a quiz or test to assess students' knowledge of key vocabulary, concepts, and principles covered in the lesson.

By incorporating engaging activities, hands-on exploration, and differentiated instruction, this lesson plan aims to deepen students' understanding of the impact of light and sound on our environment and empower them to take meaningful action to mitigate pollution.

Closure

Throughout the lesson series, encourage students to reflect on their learning by revisiting the KWL chart and discussing what they have learned about light and sound. Emphasize the interconnectedness of science with other subjects and its relevance to their everyday lives.
