Introduction to Light and Sound (Science, Language Arts- Oral Communication)

  Big Ideas:

  • Light and sounds are forms of energy with specific properties
  • Technological innovations involving light and sound have an impact on the environment
 Learning Objectives:
The students will be able to:
  • Identify sources of light in and around the school
  • Classify sources of light into two categories: natural and artificial
  • Interesting facts about light and sound
Specific Expectations:
  • Identify a variety of natural and artificial light sources
  • Distinguish between objects and living things that emit their own light and those that reflect light from other sources
Cross Curricular Connections:

English language art -Conditional statements

  Instructional Strategies:
Observation, Recording data, Group discussions and peer teaching to encourage collaboration and communication skills

Learning Activities

Engagement:

Begin with a KWL (Know, Want to know, Learned) chart about light and sound. Discuss prior knowledge and generate questions.

Conditional statement- Worksheet

Exploration:

Conduct hands-on experiments to explore the properties of light and sound. For example, use prisms to separate white light into its component colors, and use tuning forks to demonstrate sound vibrations.

Work sheets-

  • Tuning Fork-what causes sound
  • Making rainbows with prism
Explanation:

Guide students in discussing their observations and findings. Introduce vocabulary words such as wavelength, frequency, reflection, and refraction.

Extension:

Have students write a short paragraph comparing and contrasting light and sound based on their observations. Natural and artificial light- Using conditional statement-For example- If the light source is manmade, it is artificial, else, it's natural.

Differentiation

Universal Design for Learning (UDL): Provide visual aids and manipulatives for students with different learning styles. Offer multiple means of representation by incorporating videos or multimedia presentations.
