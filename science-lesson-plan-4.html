Applications of Light (Science, Art)

  Big Ideas

  • Apply the creative process to produce a variety of two- and three-dimensional art works, using elements, principles, and techniques of visual arts to communicate feelings, ideas, and understandings
  • Technological innovations involving light and sound have an impact on the environment
 Curriculum Standards

Obtain, evaluate, and communicate information about the nature of light and how light interacts with objects

 Art Standards

Develop life skills through the study and production of art (e.g. collaboration, creativity, critical thinking, and communication).

 Learning Objectives:
The students will be able to:
  • Describe properties of sound
  • Explain how sound travels through waves and is transferred into different types of matter
  • Describe the pattern in which sound waves travel
Specific Expectations:
  • Describe properties of sound, including that sound travels through a medium as a wave and that a sound can be absorbed, reflected and modified
  • Describe how different objects and materials interact with light and sound energy
Cross Curricular Connections:

Visual Art

  Instructional Strategies:
Hands-on activities-creating a model, observation, visual- aids like video, recording and group discussions

Learning Activities

Engagement:

Show students examples of artworks that utilize light as a primary element, such as installations by James Turrell or light sculptures by Olafur Eliasson. Discuss how light can be used as a medium of expression in art and its importance in enhancing visual impact.

“LIGHT is this thing we usually use to illuminate other things. I'm interested that light has thingness itself, so it's not something that reveals something about other things you're looking at, but it becomes a revelation in itself.” - James Turrell

Exploration:

Provide hands-on exploration stations where students can experiment with different light sources and materials to create their own light art pieces. Encourage them to play with shadows, colors, and reflections to express their creativity.

Explanation:

Lead a class discussion on the scientific principles behind light and its interaction with objects. Introduce vocabulary such as reflection, refraction, opaque, transparent, and translucent. Light_investigation- Worksheet

Show a video titled "Liter of Light" to illustrate how light refraction is used in 3rd World countries to reduce electricity costs for families. Discuss how light refraction works and its practical applications.

Extension:

Challenge students to apply their understanding of light refraction by creating a light box. Demonstrate how to create a light box using a big box, plastic water bottles, duct tape, and scissors. Guide students through the steps of filling the bottles with water, tracing and cutting holes in the box, covering the holes with foil, and arranging the bottles inside the box to observe light refraction.

Allow students to customize their light boxes with optional materials like paint or food coloring.

Differentiation

UDL: Provide visual aids and models to support understanding of scientific concepts. Offer choice in materials and techniques for creating light art to accommodate different learning preferences.

ELL: Use visual demonstrations and hands-on activities to illustrate abstract concepts. Provide bilingual glossaries or vocabulary cards for scientific and artistic terms related to light.

Light_sound colour- Worksheet

Offer additional support and scaffolding during hands- on activities. Provide modified tasks or alternative materials for students with sensory sensitivities or motor challenges.


Assessments
Formative:

Observe student engagement and participation during hands-on activities. Use informal assessments such as questioning and peer feedback to gauge understanding of scientific principles and artistic concepts.

Summative:

Evaluate students' light boxes based on creativity, technical skill, and incorporation of scientific principles. Use a rubric to assess students' ability to demonstrate light refraction and explain its effects.

Shadow and light- Worksheet

Conlusion

Conclude the lesson by discussing the importance of light in art and science. Review the scientific concepts of reflection and refraction and how they were applied in creating light art. Encourage students to reflect on their learning experiences and consider how they can apply their knowledge of light in future creative endeavors.
